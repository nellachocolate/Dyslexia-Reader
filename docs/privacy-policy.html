Dyslexia Reader

Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 20, 2026

Dyslexia Reader is a browser extension for Chrome and Edge that provides accessibility reading tools on webpages. This policy explains what information the extension processes, how it is used, and when it is shared.

What the extension does

Dyslexia Reader helps users read webpage text more easily through features such as hover dictionary, sentence highlighting with optional text-to-speech, syllable display, text resizing, line and word spacing controls, and AI rewrite of user-selected text.

Information processed locally in your browser

When you turn the extension on for a page, Dyslexia Reader may read and modify visible webpage text locally in your browser in order to:

  • highlight a sentence you select,
  • read the sentence aloud using the browser's text-to-speech features when that option is enabled,
  • show syllable breaks,
  • change text size, and
  • adjust line spacing and word spacing, and
  • display interface elements such as tooltips and overlays.

This local page processing is used only to provide the extension's reading-support features.

Information stored on your device

Dyslexia Reader stores your settings locally using your browser's extension storage. These settings can include:

  • feature toggles,
  • text size preferences,
  • line spacing and word spacing preferences,
  • text-to-speech settings,
  • preferred AI model, and
  • your OpenAI API key, if you choose to enter one.

This information is stored locally in your browser and is not sent to the developer.

Information sent to third-party services

Dyslexia Reader does not operate its own backend server. When needed for a user-requested feature, the extension sends limited data directly from your browser to the following third-party services:

  • Free Dictionary API (`dictionaryapi.dev`): when hover dictionary is enabled and you hover over a word, the extension may send that word to retrieve a definition.
  • OpenAI (`api.openai.com`): when you explicitly use the AI rewrite feature, the extension sends only the text you selected, along with your chosen model setting and your API key, to OpenAI so the text can be rewritten in simpler language.

How information is used

The extension uses processed information only to provide the reading-support features requested by the user. The extension is not designed to sell personal data, build advertising profiles, or track browsing for marketing purposes.

How information is shared

Information is shared only when necessary to provide a feature you use:

  • a hovered word may be sent to the dictionary service for a definition, and
  • selected text may be sent to OpenAI for AI rewrite when you request that feature.

Other than those direct feature-related requests, Dyslexia Reader does not sell or share your data with advertisers, data brokers, or analytics providers.

Data retention

The extension stores settings locally until you change them, clear browser extension storage, or uninstall the extension. Data sent to third-party services is subject to those services' own privacy practices and retention policies.

Security

Dyslexia Reader uses HTTPS requests for external API calls. You should keep your OpenAI API key private and only enter it into copies of the extension you trust.

Your choices

  • You can leave the extension turned off on any page.
  • You can avoid using AI rewrite if you do not want selected text sent to OpenAI.
  • You can remove your OpenAI API key from the extension settings at any time.
  • You can uninstall the extension to remove its local storage from your browser.

Children

Dyslexia Reader is not directed to children under 13, and the developer does not knowingly collect personal information from children through the extension.

Changes to this policy

This privacy policy may be updated from time to time. If material changes are made, the revised version will be posted at the same policy URL with an updated effective date.
